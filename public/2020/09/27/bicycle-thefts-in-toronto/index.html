Bicycle thefts in Toronto

2020-09-27

Abstraction

Since bicycles entered the human history, they have been helping improve human beings’ lives in many aspects. They offer a cheaper, healthier way for people to travel around the city and provides a solution to better environments. Therefore, It is very necessary to understand which part of the city has occurred more bicycle thefts and we need to be careful when entering these areas so as to reduce the chances of getting our friend stolen.

Introduction

As people have noticed, bicycles are playing a very important role in our lives. We use them for commuting, cardio exercising and even sightseeing. Therefore, it is necessary to protect our bicycles from incidents like being stolen. Besides physical protection like stronger bicycle locks, I feel like we need to know a little knowledge about which areas of the city that bicycles are more likely to be stolen so as we should be more cautious when entering these areas.

To start with, I use a data set called “Bicycle Thefts” from the open data portal of Toronto to help with my study. This data set records information of all the bicycle thefts reported from year 2014 to 2019. Since I am more interested in the areas where the thefts occur, I choose to make a bar plot with information under “Division” section. It is surprising that there were more bicycle theft events happening in division 14, 51 and 52, with nearly 4000 counts respectively. By checking the “Toronto Police Service” website, I figure out that these three divisions cover almost the entire downtown area of Toronto, which means we should always be more careful when leaving our bicycles alone in downtown area. Also, I plot the bar plot of the colors of the stolen bikes to see if there is a relation. The result shows there are several colors which appear way more often than others, which means there is a relation between stolen bikes and their colors.

In general, in order to protect our bicycles better, we should always be careful when leaving them in downtown area, even for a short amount of time as less than 10 percent of these stolen bicycles have been recovered according to the police. The weakness of this study is that there may be bicycle thefts not reported to the police or people may not inform the police whether they find their bicycle, which leads to an influence on accuracy of the data. In the future, people should look after their bikes better with stronger protections since bicycles improve our life qualities.
